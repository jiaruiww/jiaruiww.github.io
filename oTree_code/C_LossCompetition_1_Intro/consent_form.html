Informed Consent Form


Introduction

Please read this form carefully. The purpose of this form is to provide you with important information about taking part in a research study. If you have any questions about the research or any portion of this form, please ask us. Participation in research is voluntary, which means that it is something for which you volunteer. It is your choice to participate in the study, or not to participate. If you choose to participate now, you may change your mind and stop participating later. If you decide not to participate, that decision will not result in any penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

The person in charge of this study is Jiarui Wang (PhD student, principal investigator) and Ray Fisman (faculty advisor). Jiarui Wang can be reached at jiaruiw@bu.edu. Ray Fisman can be reached at rfisman@bu.edu. We will refer to these people as the “researchers” throughout this form.


Why is this study being done?

The purpose of this study is to examine people’s decision-making behavior. We are asking you to take part in this study because you are an adult residing in the United States and are registered on Prolific. Approximately 800 participants will take part in this research study on Prolific.


Who is Funding the Study?

The study is funded by Department of Economics at Boston University. There are no investigators that have any special interests or conflicts of interests related to the research.


Procedures

We expect that you will be in this research study for approximately 15–25 minutes. During this time, we will ask you to complete an IQ quiz and then answer a set of survey questions.

Please note that there is a part of this study that is not being disclosed to you at this time. This is necessary because revealing full details now could compromise the integrity of the research results. After you complete all study procedures, you will be provided with a full explanation of the study. At that point, you will have the opportunity to decide whether you would like your data to be included in the research or withdrawn.


What are the risks of taking part in this research study?

Risks of Completing Tasks and Survey Risks: You may feel tired after completing the IQ quiz. You can quit the study anytime if you feel uncomfortable. However, if you choose to quit, your participation will be terminated, and you may not be able to restart the survey. Loss of Confidentiality: This study is completely anonymous. Prolific assigns unique, de-identified participant IDs to ensure privacy. No personally identifiable information will be collected during the study. The main risk of allowing us to use and store your information for research is a potential loss of privacy. We will protect your privacy by storing the data in a password-protected computer. Any people other than principal investigator and co-investigator cannot get access to the data, except the case where parties like IRB or Central University Offices require to review the information for quality control or safety purpose.


Are there any benefits from being in this research study?

There are no direct benefits from this study.


How will you keep my study records confidential?

The study data will be stored in the researchers’ password-protected computer. The results of this research study may be published or used for teaching. We will not include identifiable information on data that are used for these purposes. This study is completely anonymous. Prolific assigns unique, de-identified participant IDs to ensure privacy. No personally identifiable information will be collected during the study.

The following people or groups may review your study records for purposes such as quality control or safety:

  • The researchers and any member of their research team
  • The Institutional Review Board at Boston University. The Institutional Review Board is a group of people who review human research studies for safety and protection of people who take part in the studies.
  • The sponsor or funding agency for this study
  • Federal and state agencies that oversee or review research
  • Central University Offices

Will I get paid for taking part in this research study?

The base payment for participating in the study is $6.00. You may lose up to $4.00 of this amount in the first section. Bonuses are available in later sections. Your final payment will depend on your performance and decisions in the study.


What will it cost me to take part in this research study?

There are no costs to you for taking part in this research study.


Who do I ask if I have questions or concerns about this research study?

Please email us with any concerns or questions about the research, or any research-related problems, during office hours from 9am to 5pm Eastern Time.

  • Jiarui Wang (PhD student, principal investigator) - email: jiaruiw@bu.edu
  • Ray Fisman (faculty advisor) - email: rfisman@bu.edu

If you have questions about your rights as a research participant, or if you have any complaints or concerns and want to speak with someone independent of the research team, you may contact the Boston University Charles River Campus IRB at 617-358-6115. The IRB Office webpage has information where you can learn more about being a participant in research, and you can also complete a Participant Feedback Survey.
